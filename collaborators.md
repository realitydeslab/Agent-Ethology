# Potential Collaborators: Agent Ethology

---

### 1. Iyad Rahwan
- **Affiliation**: Max Planck Institute for Human Development, Berlin
- **Key papers**: "Machine Behaviour" (Nature, 2019)
- **Collaboration angle**: Intellectual godfather of the "machine behaviour" paradigm. Collaboration would provide legitimacy and connect us to the original framework we extend. Could co-author a perspective piece or serve as workshop keynote.

### 2. Ricard Solé
- **Affiliation**: Universitat Pompeu Fabra, Barcelona; Santa Fe Institute
- **Key papers**: "How Turing parasites expand the computational landscape of digital life" (PRE, 2023)
- **Collaboration angle**: Leading expert on digital parasitism and ALife ecology. His mathematical frameworks for host-parasite coevolution directly apply to our parasitic agent category.

### 3. Joon Sung Park
- **Affiliation**: Stanford University
- **Key papers**: "Generative Agents: Interactive Simulacra of Human Behavior" (UIST, 2023)
- **Collaboration angle**: Creator of the generative agents framework. Could extend Smallville sandbox to study wild agent behaviors, or help design observation methodologies.

### 4. The Anh Han
- **Affiliation**: Teesside University, UK
- **Key papers**: "Emergent behaviours in multi-agent systems with evolutionary game theory" (2022)
- **Collaboration angle**: Evolutionary game theory expertise for modeling agent interactions. Could formalize our taxonomic categories using EGT.

### 5. Alexander Ororbia
- **Affiliation**: Rochester Institute of Technology
- **Key papers**: "Mortal Computation: A Foundation for Biomimetic Intelligence" (with Friston, 2023)
- **Collaboration angle**: Co-creator of the mortal computation framework. Direct connection to our mortal/mortal-aware agent categories.

### 6. Primavera De Filippi
- **Affiliation**: CNRS / Harvard Berkman Klein Center
- **Key papers**: "Decentralized autonomous organizations" (JAIS, 2021); blockchain governance research
- **Collaboration angle**: Expert on DAOs and autonomous governance on blockchain. Could help frame sovereign agents and soulbound agent identity from a governance perspective.

### 7. Philip Bontrager / Kenneth O. Stanley
- **Affiliation**: Lila Sciences (Stanley); various (Bontrager)
- **Key papers**: Open-ended evolution, novelty search, AI-generating algorithms
- **Collaboration angle**: Open-ended evolution expertise directly relevant to studying how agent behaviors evolve in the wild. Stanley keynoted ALIFE 2025.

### 8. Luís F. Seoane
- **Affiliation**: Centro Nacional de Biotecnología, Madrid
- **Key papers**: "How Turing parasites expand the computational landscape" (with Solé, 2023)
- **Collaboration angle**: Mathematical modeling of digital parasitism; could help formalize parasite-host dynamics for blockchain agent ecosystems.

### 9. Manuel Cebrian
- **Affiliation**: Max Planck Institute (co-author with Rahwan)
- **Key papers**: "Machine Behaviour" (Nature, 2019); social dynamics research
- **Collaboration angle**: Expertise in social dynamics and collective intelligence; relevant for studying agent societies and emergent coordination.

### 10. Emily Dolson
- **Affiliation**: Michigan State University
- **Key papers**: Work on digital evolution, Avida; Best Paper at ALIFE 2025 ("Partner Choice Can Enable Parasite Exclusion in Endosymbiotic Coevolution")
- **Collaboration angle**: Her ALIFE 2025 best paper on parasite exclusion in coevolution is directly relevant. Active in the ALIFE community.
