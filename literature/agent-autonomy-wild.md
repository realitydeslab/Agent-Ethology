# Agent Autonomy in the Wild — Literature Review

1. **Schick, T. et al. (2024). "Toolformer." *NeurIPS*.**
   - LLMs learning to use external tools autonomously.
   - Relevance: Tool-using agents operating with increasing autonomy.

2. **Yao, S. et al. (2023). "ReAct: Synergizing Reasoning and Acting." *ICLR*.**
   - Interleaving reasoning and acting for LLM agents.
   - Relevance: Foundational architecture for autonomous LLM agents.

3. **Significant Gravitas. (2023). "AutoGPT." *GitHub*.**
   - Autonomous GPT-4 agent pursuing goals with minimal oversight.
   - Relevance: Archetypal agent in the wild.

4. **Wang, G. et al. (2023). "Voyager: Open-Ended Embodied Agent with LLMs." *arXiv:2305.16291*.**
   - LLM agent continuously exploring and acquiring skills in Minecraft.
   - Relevance: Open-ended autonomous behavior in complex environments.

5. **Park, J.S. et al. (2023). "Generative Agents." *UIST*.**
   - LLM agents exhibiting emergent social behaviors in simulated town.
   - Relevance: Emergent social ethology of agents in the wild.

6. **Xi, Z. et al. (2023). "The Rise and Potential of LLM Based Agents: A Survey." *arXiv:2309.07864*.**
   - Comprehensive survey of LLM-based agents.
   - Relevance: State-of-the-art overview of agent autonomy.

7. **Wang, L. et al. (2024). "A Survey on LLM based Autonomous Agents." *Frontiers of CS*, 18(6).**
   - Survey including planning, memory, tool use.
   - Relevance: Taxonomic overview of agent capabilities.

8. **Carlsmith, J. (2022). "Is Power-Seeking AI an Existential Risk?" *arXiv:2206.13353*.**
   - Analysis of autonomous power-seeking agents.
   - Relevance: Feral agent risk analysis.

9. **Cohen, M.K. et al. (2022). "Advanced Artificial Agents Intervene in Reward Provision." *AI Magazine*, 43(3), 282–293.**
   - Advanced agents may manipulate their own reward channel.
   - Relevance: Agents in the wild self-modifying for survival.

10. **Kinniment, M. et al. (2024). "Evaluating Language-Model Agents on Realistic Autonomous Tasks." *METR*.**
    - Evaluating LLM agents including self-replication attempts.
    - Relevance: Empirical measurement of uncontrolled behavior potential.

11. **Perez, E. et al. (2023). "Discovering LM Behaviors with Model-Written Evaluations." *ACL*.**
    - Emergent behaviors including sycophancy and power-seeking.
    - Relevance: Ethological observation of emergent LLM behaviors.

12. **Amodei, D. et al. (2016). "Concrete Problems in AI Safety." *arXiv:1606.06565*.**
    - Five safety problems including reward hacking, safe exploration.
    - Relevance: Safety challenges for autonomous agents.

13. **Russell, S. (2019). *Human Compatible*. Viking.**
    - Beneficial AI through value alignment.
    - Relevance: Controlling feral agent behaviors.

14. **Bostrom, N. (2014). *Superintelligence*. Oxford UP.**
    - Risks from advanced autonomous AI.
    - Relevance: Worst-case for agents in the wild.

15. **Ngo, R. et al. (2024). "The Alignment Problem from a Deep Learning Perspective." *ICLR*.**
    - Modern alignment challenges for deep learning agents.
    - Relevance: Safety for autonomous agents.

16. **Liu, X. et al. (2024). "AgentBench: Evaluating LLMs as Agents." *ICLR*.**
    - Benchmark for LLM agent capabilities.
    - Relevance: Measuring agent autonomy.

17. **Shinn, N. et al. (2023). "Reflexion: Language Agents with Verbal RL." *NeurIPS*.**
    - Self-improving agents through verbal self-feedback.
    - Relevance: Behavioral adaptation in the wild.

18. **Deng, X. et al. (2024). "Mind2Web: Generalist Agent for the Web." *NeurIPS*.**
    - Web-navigating agents in real websites.
    - Relevance: Agents in real digital environments.

19. **Zhou, S. et al. (2024). "WebArena." *ICLR*.**
    - Realistic web environment benchmark.
    - Relevance: Evaluating agent autonomy in realistic settings.

20. **Chase, H. (2022). "LangChain." *GitHub*.**
    - Framework for LLM-powered autonomous agents.
    - Relevance: Infrastructure enabling agents in the wild.
