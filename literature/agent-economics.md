# Agent Economics — Literature Review

1. **Hurwicz, L. (1973). "The Design of Mechanisms for Resource Allocation." *AER*, 63(2), 1–30.**
   - Foundational mechanism design theory.
   - Relevance: Designing economic rules for agent ecosystems.

2. **Myerson, R.B. (1981). "Optimal Auction Design." *Math OR*, 6(1), 58–73.**
   - Optimal mechanisms for auction settings with strategic agents.
   - Relevance: Auction mechanisms for agent resource markets.

3. **Varian, H.R. (1995). "Economic Mechanism Design for Computerized Agents." *USENIX E-Commerce*.**
   - Early mechanism design for computational agents.
   - Relevance: Directly addresses economic mechanisms for artificial agents.

4. **Parkes, D.C. (2001). "Iterative Combinatorial Auctions." *PhD Thesis, UPenn*.**
   - Computationally efficient mechanism design.
   - Relevance: Practical mechanisms for agent trading.

5. **Buterin, V. (2014). "Ethereum White Paper."**
   - Ethereum as platform for agent-to-agent economic interactions.
   - Relevance: Infrastructure for autonomous agent economics.

6. **Christidis, K. & Devetsikiotis, M. (2016). "Blockchains and Smart Contracts for IoT." *IEEE Access*, 4, 2292–2303.**
   - Smart contracts enabling automated agent transactions.
   - Relevance: Economic infrastructure for agent markets.

7. **Kahneman, D. & Tversky, A. (1979). "Prospect Theory." *Econometrica*, 47(2), 263–291.**
   - Behavioral economics of risk; reference-dependent preferences.
   - Relevance: Risk perception for agent decision-making.

8. **Sandholm, T. (1999). "Distributed Rational Decision Making." In *Multiagent Systems*, 201–258.**
   - Rational decision-making in multi-agent settings.
   - Relevance: Agent risk management in ecosystems.

9. **Wellman, M.P. (2006). "Methods for Empirical Game-Theoretic Analysis." *AAAI*, 1552–1555.**
   - Empirical game theory for agent interactions.
   - Relevance: Analyzing economic risk in agent strategy spaces.

10. **Buterin, V. et al. (2019). "A Flexible Design for Funding Public Goods." *Management Science*, 65(11), 5171–5187.**
    - Quadratic funding for public goods in agent ecosystems.
    - Relevance: Mechanism for insuring collective agent welfare.

11. **Harvey, C.R. et al. (2021). *DeFi and the Future of Finance*. Wiley.**
    - DeFi including insurance protocols.
    - Relevance: DeFi insurance for insured agents.

12. **Zheng, S. et al. (2022). "The AI Economist." *Science Advances*, 8(18), eabk2607.**
    - AI agents learning optimal economic policies through multi-agent RL.
    - Relevance: Agents as economic actors and policy designers.

13. **Calvano, E. et al. (2020). "AI, Algorithmic Pricing, and Collusion." *AER*, 110(10), 3267–3297.**
    - AI agents learning tacit collusion on pricing.
    - Relevance: Emergent economic behaviors in agent markets.

14. **Easley, D. & Kleinberg, J. (2010). *Networks, Crowds, and Markets*. Cambridge UP.**
    - Network economics in interconnected agent systems.
    - Relevance: Foundation for networked agent economies.

15. **Biais, B. et al. (2019). "The Blockchain Folk Theorem." *RFS*, 32(5), 1662–1715.**
    - Game theory of blockchain consensus as economic mechanism.
    - Relevance: Economic security for on-chain agents.

16. **Cong, L.W. & He, Z. (2019). "Blockchain Disruption and Smart Contracts." *RFS*, 32(5), 1754–1797.**
    - Economic analysis of smart contracts.
    - Relevance: Theory of agent-executed contracts.

17. **Chen, Y. & Bellavitis, C. (2020). "Blockchain Disruption and DeFi." *JBVI*, 13, e00151.**
    - DeFi as infrastructure for decentralized agent activity.
    - Relevance: Financial instruments for autonomous agents.

18. **Marchetti, R. (2020). "Digital Inheritance and Digital Assets." *ERPL*, 28(5), 1035–1058.**
    - Legal framework for digital asset inheritance.
    - Relevance: Legal foundation for inherited agent transfers.

19. **Horton, J.J. (2023). "Homo Silicus." *NBER Working Paper 31122*.**
    - LLMs as economic agents; behavioral economics with AI.
    - Relevance: AI agents as subjects of behavioral economic study.

20. **Shyam Sundar, S. & Kim, J. (2019). "Machine Heuristic." *CHB*, 97, 154–163.**
    - Trust heuristics for computational agents.
    - Relevance: Trust economics in agent markets.
