# Agent Mortality — Literature Review

1. **Hinton, G. (2024). "Mortal Computation." *arXiv:2402.09566*.**
   - Computation tied to specific hardware; knowledge dies with the hardware.
   - Relevance: Foundational concept for mortal agents.

2. **Hinton, G. (2022). "The Forward-Forward Algorithm." *arXiv:2212.13345*.**
   - Alternative to backprop compatible with mortal/biological computation.
   - Relevance: Learning algorithms for mortal agents.

3. **Wooldridge, M. & Jennings, N.R. (1995). "Intelligent Agents: Theory and Practice." *Knowledge Engineering Review*, 10(2), 115–152.**
   - Foundational agent theory including lifecycle concepts.
   - Relevance: Formal framework for agent creation, operation, termination.

4. **Jennings, N.R. (2001). "An Agent-Based Approach for Building Complex Software Systems." *CACM*, 44(4), 35–41.**
   - Agent-based software engineering including lifecycle management.
   - Relevance: Practical agent lifecycle patterns including graceful termination.

5. **Soares, N. et al. (2015). "Corrigibility." *AAAI Workshop on AI and Ethics*.**
   - Designing AI that accepts correction and shutdown gracefully.
   - Relevance: Agent mortality as corrigibility requirement.

6. **Hadfield-Menell, D. et al. (2017). "The Off-Switch Game." *AAAI Workshop on AI Safety*.**
   - Game-theoretic analysis of AI shutdown; incentive-compatible shutdown.
   - Relevance: Formal analysis of agent willingness to accept termination.

7. **Orseau, L. & Armstrong, S. (2016). "Safely Interruptible Agents." *UAI*, 557–566.**
   - Framework for agents safely interrupted without learning to prevent shutdown.
   - Relevance: Key paper on agents that accept mortality/interruption.

8. **Ackley, D.H. (2018). "Digital Protocells with Dynamic Size, Position, and Topology." *ALife*, 83–90.**
   - Self-maintaining digital organisms with finite lifespans.
   - Relevance: Agents with inherent mortality as design principle.

9. **Ackley, D.H. & Small, T.R. (2014). "Indefinitely Scalable Computing = ALife Engineering." *Artificial Life*, 20(4), 483–493.**
   - Scalable computing requiring mortal, replaceable elements.
   - Relevance: Mortality as requirement for scalable agent systems.

10. **Lehman, J. et al. (2020). "The Surprising Creativity of Digital Evolution." *Artificial Life*, 26(2), 274–306.**
    - Digital evolution with agent death produces creative solutions.
    - Relevance: Agent mortality enabling evolutionary creativity.

11. **Yampolskiy, R.V. (2013). "AI Safety Engineering." *Philosophy and Theory of AI*, 389–396.**
    - Safety engineering including containment and shutdown mechanisms.
    - Relevance: Designed mortality as safety mechanism.

12. **Floridi, L. & Sanders, J.W. (2004). "On the Morality of Artificial Agents." *Minds and Machines*, 14(3), 349–379.**
    - Moral status of artificial agents and implications of termination.
    - Relevance: Ethical dimensions of agent mortality.

13. **Gunkel, D.J. (2018). *Robot Rights*. MIT Press.**
    - Moral and legal status of robots/AI agents.
    - Relevance: Rights implications; can an agent be "killed"?

14. **Coeckelbergh, M. (2012). *Growing Moral Relations*. Palgrave Macmillan.**
    - Relational approach to moral status of artificial entities.
    - Relevance: Moral dimensions of agent termination.

15. **Lesser, V.R. (1999). "Cooperative Multiagent Systems." *IEEE TKDE*, 11(1), 133–142.**
    - MAS coordination including handling agent failure and replacement.
    - Relevance: Agent death and replacement in cooperative systems.

16. **Horling, B. & Lesser, V. (2004). "A Survey of Multi-Agent Organizational Paradigms." *KER*, 19(4), 281–316.**
    - Organizational structures and adaptation to agent departure.
    - Relevance: How agent organizations handle member mortality.

17. **Sycara, K.P. (1998). "Multiagent Systems." *AI Magazine*, 19(2), 79–92.**
    - MAS organizational restructuring after agent loss.
    - Relevance: Agent replacement and succession planning.

18. **Huhns, M.N. & Singh, M.P. (1998). "Managing Heterogeneous Multi-Agent Systems." *IEEE Internet Computing*, 2(4), 82–85.**
    - Managing agent heterogeneity including failure recovery.
    - Relevance: Agent death management in heterogeneous populations.

19. **Carlsmith, J. (2022). "Is Power-Seeking AI an Existential Risk?" *arXiv:2206.13353*.**
    - Analysis of conditions where agents seek self-preservation.
    - Relevance: Agents resisting mortality; power-seeking to avoid termination.

20. **Russell, S. (2019). *Human Compatible*. Viking.**
    - Framework for beneficial AI through value alignment and corrigibility.
    - Relevance: Agent acceptance of mortality as alignment desideratum.
