# AI Behavioral Science — Literature Review

1. **Rahwan, I. et al. (2019). "Machine Behaviour." *Nature*, 568, 477–486.**
   - Proposed "machine behaviour" as interdisciplinary field.
   - Relevance: THE seminal paper for Agent Ethology.

2. **Bonnefon, J.-F. et al. (2016). "The Social Dilemma of Autonomous Vehicles." *Science*, 352, 1573–1576.**
   - Moral Machine experiment; public preferences for AI decisions.
   - Relevance: Behavioral science of AI moral choices.

3. **Awad, E. et al. (2018). "The Moral Machine Experiment." *Nature*, 563, 59–64.**
   - Largest study of moral preferences for machine behavior across cultures.
   - Relevance: Cross-cultural expectations for agent norms.

4. **Nass, C. & Moon, Y. (2000). "Machines and Mindlessness." *J. Social Issues*, 56(1), 81–103.**
   - Humans apply social rules to computers (CASA paradigm).
   - Relevance: Humans treating agents as social beings.

5. **Reeves, B. & Nass, C. (1996). *The Media Equation*. Cambridge UP.**
   - People treat computers as real social actors.
   - Relevance: Foundation for studying agent social behavior.

6. **Wei, J. et al. (2022). "Emergent Abilities of Large Language Models." *TMLR*.**
   - Emergent capabilities at scale in LLMs.
   - Relevance: Emergent agent behaviors as ethological phenomena.

7. **Bubeck, S. et al. (2023). "Sparks of AGI." *arXiv:2303.12712*.**
   - Comprehensive behavioral analysis of GPT-4.
   - Relevance: Behavioral observation and cataloging; ethological field notes.

8. **Shanahan, M. et al. (2023). "Role Play with LLMs." *Nature*, 623, 493–498.**
   - LLMs as role-playing engines; behavior depends on persona.
   - Relevance: Agent behavioral plasticity; persona as ecological niche.

9. **Perez, E. et al. (2023). "Discovering LM Behaviors." *ACL*.**
   - Automated discovery of sycophancy, power-seeking in LLMs.
   - Relevance: Systematic behavioral ethology of LLM agents.

10. **Fong, T. et al. (2003). "A Survey of Socially Interactive Robots." *RAS*, 42(3-4), 143–166.**
    - Social robot behaviors and human-robot interaction.
    - Relevance: Behavioral repertoires of social agents.

11. **Dautenhahn, K. (2007). "Socially Intelligent Robots." *Phil Trans R Soc B*, 362, 679–704.**
    - Framework for socially intelligent robot behavior.
    - Relevance: Social intelligence as behavioral ecology.

12. **Hancock, P.A. et al. (2011). "Meta-Analysis of Trust in Human-Robot Interaction." *Human Factors*, 53(5), 517–527.**
    - Meta-analysis of trust factors in HRI.
    - Relevance: Trust as behavioral dynamic in human-agent ecology.

13. **Silver, D. et al. (2016). "Mastering Go with DNNs and Tree Search." *Nature*, 529, 484–489.**
    - AlphaGo's novel behavioral strategies (Move 37).
    - Relevance: Emergent creative behaviors; novel repertoires.

14. **Brown, N. & Sandholm, T. (2019). "Superhuman AI for Multiplayer Poker." *Science*, 365, 885–890.**
    - Pluribus: superhuman poker AI with bluffing and deception.
    - Relevance: Deceptive behavioral strategies in agents.

15. **Hagendorff, T. et al. (2023). "Human-Like Intuitive Behavior in LLMs." *Nature Computational Science*, 3, 833–838.**
    - LLMs exhibit cognitive biases similar to humans.
    - Relevance: Cognitive ethology; behavioral biases as ethological traits.

16. **Argyle, L.P. et al. (2023). "Out of One, Many." *Political Analysis*, 31(3), 337–351.**
    - LLMs simulate diverse human behavioral patterns.
    - Relevance: Agent behavioral diversity.

17. **Aher, G.V. et al. (2023). "Using LLMs to Simulate Multiple Humans." *ICML*.**
    - LLMs replicating classic psychology experiments.
    - Relevance: Agent behavioral science replicating human findings.

18. **Horton, J.J. (2023). "Homo Silicus." *NBER Working Paper 31122*.**
    - LLMs as economic agents.
    - Relevance: AI agents as behavioral economic subjects.

19. **Mou, Y. & Xu, K. (2017). "The Media Inequality." *CHB*, 72, 432–440.**
    - Differences in social behavior toward AI vs humans.
    - Relevance: Behavioral ecology of human-agent interactions.

20. **Völkel, S.T. et al. (2020). "Chatbot Personality." *CONVERSATIONS*, 119–136.**
    - Measuring perceived chatbot personality (Big Five).
    - Relevance: Agent personality as behavioral phenotype.
