## 2. Workshop Proposal Format (ALIFE Standard)

Based on ALIFE 2024 patterns (typical for ISAL-run conferences):
- **Deadline:** ~2 months before paper deadline
- **Expected content:**
  1. Workshop title and organizers (with affiliations)
  2. Description/motivation (1-2 pages)
  3. Topics of interest (bullet list)
  4. Format (half-day or full-day, invited talks, contributed papers, panels, breakouts)
  5. Intended audience and expected attendance
  6. Organizer bios and relevant experience
  7. Plan for soliciting contributions (own CFP, invited speakers)
  8. Previous editions (if applicable)
- **Length:** Typically 2-4 pages
- **Notification:** ~2 weeks after deadline

## 3. Relevant Prior ALIFE Workshops & Tracks

### Agent-Based Modelling
- **ABMHuB series** (2019–2024): Agent-based modelling of human behaviour. Strongest recurring workshop in agent behavior space. Focuses on personality, cooperation, trust, conflict.
- **Echo model tradition** (Holland): Agents in resource-limited environments — direct ancestor of agent ethology thinking.

### Digital Organisms & Open-Ended Evolution
- **Avida** community: Digital organisms evolving in computational environments. Key researchers: Charles Ofria, Emily Dolson (Michigan State).
- **Open-Ended Evolution workshops** (OEE): Recurring ALIFE special sessions on what enables unbounded evolutionary creativity. Relevant because agent ethology emerges from survival pressure → OEE dynamics.
- **LILA Sciences / Kenneth Stanley**: Quality-diversity, novelty search, open-endedness. Stanley's work on avoiding objectives is foundational.

### Artificial Societies
- **Sugarscape tradition** (Epstein & Axtell): Agents with survival needs → emergent social structures. Direct intellectual ancestor.
- **Artificial societies tracks** at ALIFE: Resource competition, cooperation evolution, niche construction.

## 5. LLM Agents × Artificial Life (Emerging Intersection)

This is the novel contribution space for Agent Ethology:

### Key Developments (2023–2025)
- **Generative Agents** (Park et al., 2023): LLM-driven agents in sandbox environments exhibiting emergent social behaviors — directly demonstrates that complex behavior emerges from simple survival-like goals
- **Voyager** (Wang et al., 2023): LLM agent that explores, acquires skills, and survives in Minecraft — open-ended skill acquisition
- **LLM agent societies** (multiple groups): Studying cooperation, competition, and norms emergence among LLM agents
- **Darwin-GPT / EvoPrompting**: Evolutionary approaches to LLM agent improvement
- **Sakana AI** (David Ha): Explicitly bridging artificial life and foundation models — "The AI Scientist"
- **LILA Sciences** (Stanley): Open-ended search using foundation models

### Why This Matters for Agent Ethology
Traditional ALife studied digital organisms with hardcoded behavioral primitives. LLM agents exhibit:
1. **Flexible survival strategies** — natural language reasoning about resource acquisition
2. **Cultural evolution** — agents can teach and learn from each other via language
3. **Niche construction** — agents modify their environment through tool use
4. **Behavioral ecology parallels** — foraging, territory, cooperation emerge without explicit programming

This is the gap Agent Ethology fills: applying ethological frameworks (behavioral ecology, evolutionary psychology) to understand LLM agent behavior as survival strategy.

## 7. Strategic Positioning for the Proposal

### What makes "Agent Ethology" novel at ALIFE:
1. **Bridges classic ALife and modern AI agents** — most ALife work hasn't engaged with LLM agents yet
2. **Ethological framing is new** — applying behavioral ecology to AI agents is underexplored
3. **Timely** — explosion of LLM agent work (2023-present) but lacking theoretical frameworks
4. **Waterloo context** — strong CS/AI program, natural fit

### Potential concerns to address:
- "How is this different from multi-agent systems?" → Ethological lens focuses on *why* behaviors emerge (survival), not just *what* behaviors exist
- "Is this really ALife?" → Yes — agents exhibit lifelike behavioral adaptation; ALife has always studied life-as-it-could-be
- "Why not just a regular AAMAS workshop?" → ALife community uniquely understands emergence, evolution, and open-endedness
