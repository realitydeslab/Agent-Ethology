# Ethology and AI — Literature Review

## Classical Ethology Foundations

1. **Lorenz, K. (1937). "The Companion in the Bird's World." *The Auk*, 54(3), 245–273.**
   - Foundational work on imprinting and fixed action patterns.
   - Relevance: Fixed action patterns as analogy for hardcoded agent behaviors.

2. **Tinbergen, N. (1963). "On Aims and Methods of Ethology." *Zeitschrift für Tierpsychologie*, 20(4), 410–433.**
   - Established the four questions of ethology: causation, development, evolution, function.
   - Relevance: Framework directly applicable to analyzing AI agent behaviors.

3. **von Frisch, K. (1967). *The Dance Language and Orientation of Bees*. Harvard University Press.**
   - Decoded the waggle dance communication system in honeybees.
   - Relevance: Communication protocols between agents; emergent signaling.

4. **Lorenz, K. (1966). *On Aggression*. Harcourt, Brace & World.**
   - Analysis of aggressive behavior patterns and their evolutionary function.
   - Relevance: Competitive agent behaviors, resource conflicts in multi-agent systems.

5. **Tinbergen, N. (1951). *The Study of Instinct*. Oxford University Press.**
   - Framework for studying innate behavior including sign stimuli and hierarchical behavior.
   - Relevance: Hierarchical behavior organization in AI agents.

## Computational Ethology

6. **Anderson, D.J. & Perona, P. (2014). "Toward a Science of Computational Ethology." *Neuron*, 84(1), 18–31.**
   - Proposed computational ethology using ML to quantify animal behavior from video.
   - Relevance: Seminal framing; methods transferable to AI agent behavior analysis.

7. **Mathis, A. et al. (2018). "DeepLabCut: Markerless Pose Estimation." *Nature Neuroscience*, 21, 1281–1289.**
   - Deep learning tool for tracking animal body parts for automated behavioral analysis.
   - Relevance: Behavioral tracking methodology applicable to agent actions in digital environments.

8. **Berman, G.J. et al. (2014). "Mapping the Stereotyped Behaviour of Freely Moving Fruit Flies." *J. Royal Society Interface*, 11(99), 20140672.**
   - Unsupervised mapping of behavioral repertoires in Drosophila.
   - Relevance: Methods for discovering behavioral repertoires in AI agents without predefined categories.

9. **Wiltschko, A.B. et al. (2015). "Mapping Sub-Second Structure in Mouse Behavior." *Neuron*, 88(6), 1121–1135.**
   - MoSeq: unsupervised discovery of behavioral syllables.
   - Relevance: Behavioral syllable concept applicable to decomposing agent action sequences.

10. **Pereira, T.D. et al. (2020). "Quantifying Behavior to Understand the Brain." *Nature Neuroscience*, 23, 1537–1549.**
    - Review of modern computational ethology methods.
    - Relevance: Comprehensive methodological review for quantifying agent behaviors.

11. **Sun, J.J. et al. (2022). "MABe22: A Multi-Agent Behavior Dataset." *NeurIPS Datasets and Benchmarks*.**
    - Large-scale multi-agent behavior dataset for computational ethology.
    - Relevance: Multi-agent behavioral analysis directly parallels multi-agent AI observation.

## Robot Ethology & Artificial Ethology

12. **Webb, B. (2001). "Can Robots Make Good Models of Biological Behaviour?" *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*, 24(6), 1033–1050.**
    - Robots as models of biological behavior.
    - Relevance: Foundational argument for cross-pollination between ethology and AI.

13. **Hallam, J. & Meyer, J.-A. (Eds.) (2002). *From Animals to Animats 7*. MIT Press.**
    - Conference series bridging animal behavior and adaptive AI/robot behavior.
    - Relevance: Long-running research program applying ethological concepts to artificial agents.

14. **Breazeal, C. (2003). "Emotion and Sociable Humanoid Robots." *IJHCS*, 59(1-2), 119–155.**
    - Sociable robot design inspired by ethological models of social behavior.
    - Relevance: Ethology-inspired agent design; social behavior repertoires.

15. **Pfeifer, R. & Scheier, C. (1999). *Understanding Intelligence*. MIT Press.**
    - Embodied cognition through agent-environment interaction.
    - Relevance: Embodied agent behavior as ethological subject.

16. **Floreano, D. & Keller, L. (2010). "Evolution of Adaptive Behaviour in Robots." *PLoS Biology*, 8(1), e1000292.**
    - Evolutionary robotics showing emergence of altruism and communication.
    - Relevance: Emergent social behaviors in artificial agents; ethological observation of evolved behaviors.

17. **Nolfi, S. & Floreano, D. (2000). *Evolutionary Robotics*. MIT Press.**
    - Comprehensive treatment of evolving robot behaviors.
    - Relevance: Evolution of behavioral repertoires in artificial agents.

## Behavioral Ecology Applied to AI

18. **Baldassarre, G. & Mirolli, M. (Eds.) (2013). *Intrinsically Motivated Learning*. Springer.**
    - Intrinsic motivation driving behavioral repertoire development.
    - Relevance: Motivation systems driving agent behavioral diversity.

19. **Oudeyer, P.-Y. & Kaplan, F. (2007). "What Is Intrinsic Motivation?" *Frontiers in Neurorobotics*, 1, 6.**
    - Taxonomy of intrinsic motivation for artificial agents.
    - Relevance: Internal drives shaping agent behavioral ecology.

20. **Seth, A.K. (2007). "The Ecology of Action Selection." *Phil Trans R Soc B*, 362(1485), 1545–1558.**
    - Ecological approach to action selection in artificial agents.
    - Relevance: Bridge between behavioral ecology and AI agent decision-making.

21. **Wilson, E.O. (1975). *Sociobiology: The New Synthesis*. Harvard University Press.**
    - Foundational sociobiology connecting evolution and social behavior.
    - Relevance: Framework for social behavior in agent populations.

22. **Scheier, C. et al. (1998). "Embedded Neural Networks." *Neural Networks*, 11(7-8), 1551–1569.**
    - How embodiment constraints shape behavioral repertoires.
    - Relevance: Environmental constraints shaping AI agent behavioral ecology.
