# Multi-Agent Ecosystems — Literature Review

1. **Ferber, J. (1999). *Multi-Agent Systems*. Addison-Wesley.**
   - Comprehensive MAS including organizational structures and emergent behavior.
   - Relevance: Foundational text for agent societies and ecosystems.

2. **Wooldridge, M. (2009). *An Introduction to MultiAgent Systems* (2nd ed.). Wiley.**
   - Standard textbook on MAS theory.
   - Relevance: Core reference for agent interaction and emergence.

3. **Reynolds, C.W. (1987). "Flocks, Herds and Schools." *ACM SIGGRAPH*, 21(4), 25–34.**
   - Boids: emergent flocking from simple local rules.
   - Relevance: Seminal demonstration of complex collective behavior from simple agent rules.

4. **Bonabeau, E. et al. (1999). *Swarm Intelligence*. Oxford UP.**
   - Swarm intelligence from social insects.
   - Relevance: Ecological perspective on multi-agent coordination.

5. **Dorigo, M. & Stützle, T. (2004). *Ant Colony Optimization*. MIT Press.**
   - Ant-inspired optimization through stigmergic coordination.
   - Relevance: Stigmergy in agent ecosystems.

6. **Holland, J.H. (1995). *Hidden Order*. Addison-Wesley.**
   - Complex adaptive systems; emergence from agent interactions.
   - Relevance: Theoretical foundation for agent ecosystem emergence.

7. **Epstein, J.M. & Axtell, R. (1996). *Growing Artificial Societies*. Brookings.**
   - Sugarscape: emergent social phenomena from agent interactions.
   - Relevance: Seminal ABM with resource competition, trade, culture.

8. **Grimm, V. & Railsback, S.F. (2005). *Individual-Based Modeling and Ecology*. Princeton UP.**
   - Individual-based ecological modeling.
   - Relevance: Methods for modeling agent ecosystems.

9. **Wilensky, U. & Rand, W. (2015). *An Introduction to Agent-Based Modeling*. MIT Press.**
   - NetLogo-based introduction to ABM.
   - Relevance: Practical toolkit for agent ecosystem modeling.

10. **Briscoe, G. & De Wilde, P. (2006). "Digital Ecosystems." *IEEE BIONETICS*, 1–6.**
    - Digital ecosystems as bio-inspired distributed environments.
    - Relevance: Ecosystem concepts applied to digital agents.

11. **Levin, S.A. (1998). "Ecosystems and the Biosphere as Complex Adaptive Systems." *Ecosystems*, 1(5), 431–436.**
    - Ecosystems as complex adaptive systems.
    - Relevance: Viewing agent systems as ecosystems.

12. **Castelfranchi, C. (1998). "Modelling Social Action for AI Agents." *AI*, 103(1-2), 157–182.**
    - Social action theory for artificial agents.
    - Relevance: Social structure and norms in agent ecosystems.

13. **Conte, R. & Paolucci, M. (2002). *Reputation in Artificial Societies*. Kluwer.**
    - Reputation dynamics and social order.
    - Relevance: Social mechanisms maintaining ecosystem stability.

14. **Panait, L. & Luke, S. (2005). "Cooperative Multi-Agent Learning." *AAMAS*, 11(3), 387–434.**
    - Survey of cooperative learning in MAS.
    - Relevance: How agents learn to cooperate in ecosystems.

15. **Lowe, R. et al. (2017). "Multi-Agent Actor-Critic (MADDPG)." *NeurIPS*, 6379–6390.**
    - Multi-agent deep RL for mixed cooperative-competitive settings.
    - Relevance: Modern approach to learning in complex agent ecosystems.

16. **Leibo, J.Z. et al. (2017). "Multi-Agent RL in Sequential Social Dilemmas." *AAMAS*, 464–473.**
    - Emergence of cooperation and competition in social dilemmas.
    - Relevance: Ecological dynamics of cooperation in agent populations.

17. **Baker, B. et al. (2020). "Emergent Tool Use From Multi-Agent Autocurricula." *ICLR*.**
    - Hide-and-seek agents developing emergent tool use.
    - Relevance: Emergent behavioral complexity from ecosystem dynamics.

18. **Cliff, D. et al. (1993). "Explorations in Evolutionary Robotics." *Adaptive Behavior*, 2(1), 73–110.**
    - Behavioral evolution in agent populations.
    - Relevance: Evolutionary dynamics shaping ecosystem diversity.

19. **DeAngelis, D.L. & Grimm, V. (2014). "Individual-Based Models in Ecology after Four Decades." *F1000Prime Reports*, 6, 39.**
    - Review of 40 years of IBM in ecology.
    - Relevance: Mature framework for studying agent ecosystems.

20. **Chang, E. & West, M. (2006). "Digital Ecosystems." *iiWAS*, 3–23.**
    - Self-organization and evolution in digital ecosystems.
    - Relevance: Ecosystem framework for self-organizing agent communities.

21. **Dignum, V. (2004). "A Model for Organizational Interaction (OperA)." *PhD Thesis, Utrecht*.**
    - Formal organizational agent models.
    - Relevance: Organizational structures in agent ecosystems.

22. **Weiss, G. (Ed.) (1999). *Multiagent Systems*. MIT Press.**
    - Multi-perspective MAS overview.
    - Relevance: Ecosystem perspective on multi-agent systems.
