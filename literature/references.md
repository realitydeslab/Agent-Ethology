# Agent Ethology Literature Review

*Compiled for ALIFE 2026 Workshop: "Agent Ethology: Studying Agents in the Wild"*

This bibliography compiles approximately 100 key references organized by thematic categories relevant to understanding AI agents operating in natural, uncontrolled environments.

## Foundational & Theoretical

### Classical Ethology & Animal Behavior
1. Tinbergen, N. (1963). On aims and methods of ethology. *Zeitschrift für Tierpsychologie*, 20(4), 410-433. — Defines the four questions framework for behavioral study.
2. Lorenz, K. (1973). *The Foundations of Ethology*. Springer-Verlag. — Foundational work on instinct and behavioral patterns.
3. Wilson, E. O. (1975). *Sociobiology: The New Synthesis*. Harvard University Press. — Framework for understanding social behavior evolution.
4. Dawkins, R. (1976). *The Selfish Gene*. Oxford University Press. — Gene-centered view of evolution and behavior.

### Machine Behavior & AI Systems
5. **Rahwan, I., Cebrian, M., Obradovich, N., et al. (2019). Machine behaviour. *Nature*, 568(7753), 477-486.** — Core reference: proposes studying AI systems as behavioral subjects.
6. Simon, H. A. (1969). *The Sciences of the Artificial*. MIT Press. — Foundational work on artificial systems and design.
7. Russell, S., & Norvig, P. (2020). *Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach* (4th ed.). Pearson. — Standard AI textbook with agent-based perspective.
8. Wooldridge, M. (2009). *An Introduction to MultiAgent Systems*. John Wiley & Sons. — Multi-agent systems fundamentals.
