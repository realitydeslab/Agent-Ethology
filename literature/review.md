## Key Seminal Works (Top 15)

1. **Rahwan et al. (2019) "Machine Behaviour"** — Nature — THE foundational paper; frames AI as subject of behavioral science
2. **Tinbergen (1963) "On Aims and Methods of Ethology"** — Four questions framework for analyzing any behavior
3. **Hinton (2024) "Mortal Computation"** — Computation tied to substrate; foundational for mortal agents
4. **Weyl, Ohlhaver & Buterin (2022) "Decentralized Society"** — Defines Soulbound Tokens for identity-bound agents
5. **Ray (1991) "Tierra"** — First digital ecosystem with parasitism, mortality, ecology
6. **Barabási et al. (2001) "Parasitic Computing"** — Seminal parasitic computation exploiting infrastructure
7. **Park et al. (2023) "Generative Agents"** — LLM agents with emergent social behaviors
8. **Epstein & Axtell (1996) "Growing Artificial Societies"** — Sugarscape: seminal agent-based ecosystem
9. **Langton (1989) "Artificial Life"** — Founded the field; life-as-it-could-be
10. **Orseau & Armstrong (2016) "Safely Interruptible Agents"** — Agents that accept mortality gracefully
11. **Baker et al. (2020) "Emergent Tool Use"** — Complex behavior from multi-agent ecosystem dynamics
12. **Maturana & Varela (1980) "Autopoiesis and Cognition"** — Self-maintaining systems; sovereign agents
13. **Axelrod (1984) "The Evolution of Cooperation"** — Cooperation vs exploitation in agent populations
14. **Anderson & Perona (2014) "Toward a Science of Computational Ethology"** — Methods for behavioral observation
15. **Carlsmith (2022) "Is Power-Seeking AI an Existential Risk?"** — Feral agent risk analysis

## Gaps the Agent Ethology Framework Addresses

1. **No unified behavioral taxonomy for AI agents** — Agent Ethology provides sovereign/parasitic/mortal/soulbound/inherited/feral/suicidal/mortal-aware/insured taxonomy
2. **Tinbergen's four questions not systematically applied to AI** — No existing work does this
3. **No ethological observation methods for digital agents** — Computational ethology tools not adapted for AI
4. **Parasitic agent behaviors understudied** in modern AI ecosystems
5. **Agent mortality not treated ethologically** — Safety/corrigibility work is disconnected from ALife mortality
6. **No bridge between ALife and LLM agent research** — Decades of relevant precedent ignored
7. **Inherited agents are conceptually novel** — No direct precedent in CS literature
8. **Insured agents are conceptually new** — Risk-hedged agents with behavioral guarantees undeveloped

## Cross-Cutting Themes

1. **Emergence is universal** — Every subdomain reports emergent phenomena
2. **Mortality-identity nexus** — Mortal computation + soulbound identity deeply connected
3. **Parasitism as ecological constant** — From Tierra (1991) to adversarial RL (2020)
4. **Autonomy-safety tension** — Every autonomy increase raises safety concerns
5. **Economic behavior bridges all categories** — Agents as economic actors cuts across all types
