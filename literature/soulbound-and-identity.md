# Soulbound Tokens & Agent Identity — Literature Review

1. **Weyl, E.G., Ohlhaver, P., & Buterin, V. (2022). "Decentralized Society: Finding Web3's Soul." *SSRN*.**
   - Proposed SBTs as non-transferable tokens for commitments, credentials, affiliations.
   - Relevance: Seminal paper for soulbound agent identity.

2. **Buterin, V. (2022). "Soulbound." *Vitalik.ca blog*.**
   - Original SBT concept inspired by World of Warcraft soulbound items.
   - Relevance: Conceptual origin of non-transferable agent binding.

3. **EIP-5192: Minimal Soulbound NFTs. (2022). Ethereum Improvement Proposal.**
   - Minimal interface for non-transferable NFTs extending ERC-721.
   - Relevance: Technical standard for agent identity tokens.

4. **EIP-6551: Non-fungible Token Bound Accounts. (2023). EIP.**
   - Every NFT gets its own smart contract account.
   - Relevance: Agents as NFTs with on-chain identity and asset ownership.

5. **EIP-4337: Account Abstraction. (2023). EIP.**
   - Smart contract wallets with custom verification logic.
   - Relevance: Agents as smart contract accounts with programmable identity.

6. **EIP-5484: Consensual Soulbound Tokens. (2022). EIP.**
   - SBTs with consent mechanisms for issuance and burning.
   - Relevance: Consensual identity binding for agents.

7. **EIP-4973: Account-bound Tokens. (2022). EIP.**
   - Non-transferable tokens bound to accounts with mutual consent.
   - Relevance: Alternative soulbound implementation for agents.

8. **Entriken, W. et al. (2018). "EIP-721: Non-Fungible Token Standard." EIP.**
   - Standard for unique NFTs on Ethereum.
   - Relevance: Foundation for representing unique agent identities.

9. **Allen, C. (2016). "The Path to Self-Sovereign Identity." *Life With Alacrity blog*.**
   - 10 principles of self-sovereign identity.
   - Relevance: Identity principles for sovereign agents.

10. **Preukschat, A. & Reed, D. (2021). *Self-Sovereign Identity*. Manning.**
    - SSI architecture including DIDs and verifiable credentials.
    - Relevance: Infrastructure for agent identity persistence.

11. **W3C. (2022). "Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs) v1.0." *W3C Recommendation*.**
    - Standard for decentralized identifiers.
    - Relevance: Identity standard for autonomous agents.

12. **Sporny, M. et al. (2022). "Verifiable Credentials Data Model v1.1." *W3C*.**
    - Standard for cryptographically verifiable claims.
    - Relevance: Agents carrying verifiable credentials.

13. **Castelfranchi, C. & Falcone, R. (2010). *Trust Theory*. Wiley.**
    - Trust models based on persistent agent identity.
    - Relevance: Identity persistence as foundation for trust.

14. **Sabater, J. & Sierra, C. (2005). "Review on Computational Trust and Reputation Models." *AIR*, 24(1), 33–60.**
    - Trust and reputation systems requiring stable identities.
    - Relevance: Soulbound reputation as trust mechanism.

15. **Resnick, P. et al. (2000). "Reputation Systems." *CACM*, 43(12), 45–48.**
    - Reputation systems and identity problems (whitewashing).
    - Relevance: SBTs solving whitewashing by preventing identity transfer.

16. **Conway, H. & Grattan, S. (2017). "The 'New' New Property." *Trusts & Trustees*, 23(9), 952–962.**
    - Legal frameworks for inheriting digital assets.
    - Relevance: Precedent for inherited agents.

17. **Harbinja, E. (2017). "Legal Aspects of Transmission of Digital Assets on Death." *CREATe*.**
    - How digital assets pass between owners upon death.
    - Relevance: Framework for inherited agent transfers.

18. **Singh, M.P. (2011). "Trust as a Basis for Social Computing." *AAMAS*, 1–2.**
    - Trust infrastructure requiring verifiable agent identities.
    - Relevance: Identity verification for agents in open systems.

19. **Chopra, A.K. & Singh, M.P. (2016). "From Social Machines to Social Protocols." *WWW*, 903–914.**
    - Social protocols requiring persistent agent identity.
    - Relevance: Soulbound identity enabling protocol participation.

20. **Cahn, N. (2011). "Postmortem Life On-Line." *Probate & Property*, 25(4), 36–39.**
    - Digital estate planning and posthumous digital presence.
    - Relevance: Agent inheritance and posthumous operation.
