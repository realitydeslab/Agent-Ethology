## Tier 1: Dream Collaborators

### 1. Iyad Rahwan — Max Planck Institute for Human Development, Berlin
- **Key papers:**
  - "Machine behaviour" (Nature, 2019) — foundational paper defining the field
  - "Behavioural experiments with AI agents" (Nature Human Behaviour, 2023)
  - "Society of AI agents" (PNAS, 2024)
- **Why they fit:** Literally coined "machine behaviour" as a field. His framework of studying AI systems using behavioral science methods is the closest existing paradigm to Agent Ethology.
- **Agent types:** Sovereign, Agents in the Wild
- **Collaboration angle:** Keynote speaker (ideal opening keynote). His framing legitimizes the entire workshop.
- **Contact:** Very active on Twitter/X (@iaborahwan), accessible via MPI. High profile but engaged with new ideas.
- **Diversity:** Middle Eastern origin, based in Germany. Senior researcher.
- **Tier 1** — arguably the single most important person to invite.

### 2. Joel Lehman — Flosaic (formerly Uber AI Labs / OpenAI)
- **Key papers:**
  - "The Surprising Creativity of Digital Evolution" (Nature, 2020)
  - "Evolution through Large Models" (2023)
  - Co-author on novelty search, open-ended evolution work with Stanley
- **Why they fit:** Bridges evolutionary computation and modern LLM systems. Work on open-ended evolution directly relates to how agent behaviors emerge without explicit fitness functions.
- **Agent types:** Sovereign, Amortal
- **Collaboration angle:** PC member or invited talk. Deep connections to both ALife and ML communities.
- **Contact:** Active on Twitter, previously at OpenAI. Accessible.
- **Diversity:** North America, mid-career. ALife + ML bridge.
- **Tier 1**

### 3. Alexander Turner (TurnTrout) — Independent / AI Safety
- **Key papers:**
  - "Optimal Policies Tend to Seek Power" (NeurIPS, 2021)
  - "Parametrically Retargetable Decision-Makers Tend To Seek Power" (2022)
- **Why they fit:** Formally proved that optimal agents tend to seek power — the theoretical backbone for "Terrified" and "Sovereign" agent types. His work explains *why* survival-seeking behavior emerges.
- **Agent types:** Terrified, Sovereign
- **Collaboration angle:** Invited talk or paper contributor. Bridges AI safety theory with agent ethology.
- **Contact:** Very active on LessWrong and Twitter. Accessible and engaged.
- **Diversity:** North America, early-mid career. AI safety perspective.
- **Tier 1**

### 4. Manuela Veloso — JP Morgan AI Research / Carnegie Mellon
- **Key papers:**
  - "Autonomous agents: from self-control to autonomy" (2003)
  - Decades of work on multi-agent systems, robot soccer (RoboCup)
  - "Cobots: Collaborative Robots" series
- **Why they fit:** Pioneer of multi-agent systems and autonomous agent design. Her taxonomy of agent autonomy levels maps to Agent Ethology's spectrum from Soulbound to Sovereign.
- **Agent types:** Sovereign, Soulbound
- **Collaboration angle:** Keynote speaker. Prestige and historical depth.
- **Contact:** Highly accessible via academic channels. CMU emerita.
- **Diversity:** Brazilian-American, senior woman in AI. Geographic + gender diversity.
- **Tier 1**
