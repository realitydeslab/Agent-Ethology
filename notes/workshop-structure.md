# Agent Ethology Workshop — Proposed Structure

## Format: Full-Day Workshop (8 hours)

Based on successful ALIFE workshop patterns (ABMHuB, ECCCI, Evolution of Things).

---

## Morning Session: Foundations (9:00–12:30)

### 9:00–9:15 — Opening & Framing
- Organizers introduce Agent Ethology as a field
- Core thesis: agent behavior emerges from survival strategy

### 9:15–10:00 — Keynote 1: "From Digital Organisms to LLM Agents"
- *Suggested speaker:* Kenneth O. Stanley or Emily Dolson
- Bridge classic ALife agent work to modern foundation model agents
- Q&A: 10 min

### 10:00–10:30 — ☕ Break

### 10:30–11:15 — Keynote 2: "Ethology Meets AI: Behavioral Ecology of Artificial Agents"
- *Suggested speaker:* Michael Levin or Josh Bongard
- How ethological frameworks (foraging theory, optimal behavior, niche construction) apply to AI agents
- Q&A: 10 min

### 11:15–12:30 — Contributed Talks Session 1 (4 × 15 min + 5 min Q&A)
- Topics: Emergent behavior in LLM agents, survival strategies in multi-agent systems, resource competition dynamics, behavioral adaptation

---

## Lunch: 12:30–14:00

---

## Afternoon Session: New Frontiers (14:00–17:30)

### 14:00–14:45 — Keynote 3: "Generative Agents and Emergent Societies"
- *Suggested speaker:* Joon Sung Park or David Ha
- LLM agent societies as ethological field sites
- Q&A: 10 min

### 14:45–15:30 — Contributed Talks Session 2 (3 × 15 min + 5 min Q&A)
- Topics: Cultural evolution in agent populations, cooperation/competition dynamics, tool use and niche construction

### 15:30–16:00 — ☕ Break

### 16:00–17:00 — Panel Discussion: "Is Agent Ethology a Science?"
- **Panelists:** 4-5 speakers + audience
- **Guiding questions:**
  1. Can we apply ethological methods (observation, classification, experimentation) to AI agents?
  2. What are the units of selection in LLM agent populations?
  3. Is "survival" a meaningful concept for digital agents? What counts as fitness?
  4. How do we distinguish genuine emergence from prompted behavior?
  5. What would a "field guide to AI agent behavior" look like?

### 17:00–17:30 — Breakout Groups & Closing
- 3 breakout topics:
  - **Methodology:** How to study agent behavior systematically
  - **Taxonomy:** Classifying agent behavioral strategies
  - **Open questions:** What do we need to build/study next?
- Brief report-backs and closing remarks

---

## Call for Contributions

### Paper types:
1. **Full papers** (8 pages) — original research
2. **Position papers** (4 pages) — provocative ideas, frameworks
3. **Demos** (2 pages + live demo) — agent behavior in action

### Topics of interest:
- Emergent behavior in LLM-based agent populations
- Survival strategies and resource acquisition in multi-agent environments
- Behavioral ecology frameworks applied to artificial agents
- Evolution of cooperation, competition, and social norms in agent societies
- Foraging theory, optimal behavior, and decision-making in AI agents
- Niche construction and environmental modification by agents
- Cultural evolution and memetic transmission between agents
- Tool use, skill acquisition, and behavioral innovation
- Ethological observation methods for AI systems
- Taxonomies of agent behavioral strategies
- Open-ended evolution of agent behavior
- Predator-prey dynamics in multi-agent systems
- Communication evolution and signaling in agent populations
