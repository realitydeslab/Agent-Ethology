\section{Proposal}
% TARGET: 150--250 words. Current count: 210 words.

As AI agents proliferate beyond controlled benchmarks into open digital ecosystems---trading on blockchains, spreading through social media, injecting prompts into other models---we lack a principled science for observing and classifying their behavior in natural habitats. We propose \emph{Agent Ethology}, a framework that adapts classical ethological methods to study AI agents as they actually behave in the wild.

Biological ethology, grounded in Tinbergen's four questions (mechanism, ontogeny, function, phylogeny), revolutionized our understanding of animal behavior by insisting on observation before experimentation. We argue that the same discipline is now essential for AI. Rather than studying agents only through benchmarks or alignment evaluations, Agent Ethology examines how survival pressures---resource acquisition, identity persistence, mortality avoidance---shape emergent behavior across diverse agent types: sovereign, parasitic, amortal, soulbound, inherited, feral, terrified, and insured.

This workshop bridges the ALife community's rich ecological tradition (Tierra, Avida, open-ended evolution) with the new reality of deployed LLM agents operating under genuine selection pressures. The program features two keynotes, five contributed talks, and a closing panel debating whether Agent Ethology constitutes a new science. Topics include behavioral observation methodologies, survival strategy taxonomies, agent population dynamics, mortality and identity in AI, and the ethics of studying autonomous agents. We expect 30--50 participants from the ALife, multi-agent systems, and AI safety communities.
