## Our Novel Contributions

### 1. First Comprehensive Ethological Taxonomy for AI Agents in the Wild
No existing work provides a unified classification system spanning 9 distinct agent behavioral types (sovereign, parasitic, mortal, soulbound, inherited, feral, suicidal, mortal-aware, insured). We draw from classical ethology to create this taxonomy.

### 2. Bridging ALife, Blockchain, and LLM Agent Communities
We uniquely connect three research communities that rarely interact:
- **ALife** (digital evolution, emergence, artificial ecosystems)
- **Blockchain/Web3** (MEV, DAOs, SBTs, autonomous on-chain agents)
- **LLM Agents** (generative agents, tool-using agents, autonomous AI)

### 3. Wild Observation Methodology for Digital Agents
While Park et al. study agents in sandboxes and Rahwan et al. propose the concept, we develop concrete methodological tools for observing agent behavior in uncontrolled digital environments (blockchains, social media, open internet).

### 4. Mortality and Identity as Behavioral Dimensions
We are the first to treat agent mortality (mortal, suicidal, mortal-aware) and identity-binding (soulbound, inherited) as fundamental behavioral dimensions within a unified framework, connecting Hinton's mortal computation to Buterin's SBTs.

### 5. Ecological Framing of Agent-Agent Interactions
Rather than studying agents in isolation, we apply ecological concepts (parasitism, predation, symbiosis, competition) to understand inter-agent dynamics in digital ecosystems.
